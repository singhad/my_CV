Research Experience

Research Project : Cosmic Rays and Atmospheric Properties 2018

Successfully completed project under Prof. (Dr.) Adrian Biland ℅ ETH Zurich , on “Monitoring the properties of the atmosphere relevant for the observation of cosmic rays and cosmic gamma rays with atmospheric Cherenkov telescopes”, where I analysed the data collected by FACT, and then proposed a method to monitor the properties of the atmosphere, not using external devices which may hinder the data taking of other nearby instruments by emitting light, but by using the telescope itself, by looking at the structure of the arrival-times (multiplicity) of single photons on the nanosecond level.

Masters' Thesis : A sub-grid model for a cosmological galaxy simulation 2018-2019

Successfully completed Masters’ Thesis titled “A sub-grid model for molecular gas in a cosmological galaxy formation simulation” under Prof. (Dr.) Romain Teyssier ℅ University of Zurich (UZH), where a model was constructed that used micro-turbulence and a lognormal probability distribution function (PDF) to describe the unresolved density fluctuations inside a macroscopic volume element. Jeans length was used to establish the length scale of the absorber for these density fluctuations. The molecular fractions of H2 and CO were computed, also taking into account the self-shielding effect of H2. Then, a simplified model for radiative transfer was introduced to compute the line emission of CO. This sub-grid model was then used to post process a cosmological zoom-in simulation to study the molecular fraction of H2 and CO, and also the line luminosity of CO. The total mass of H2 in the galaxy and the total luminosity of the galaxy were computed for the CO(1-0) transition. The results were compared to observations taken of similar galaxies, and conclusions were drawn.
